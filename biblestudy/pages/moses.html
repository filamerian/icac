Moses: Deliverance and Divine Calling (Exodus 1-4)

"Come, I will send you to Pharaoh that you may bring my people out of Egypt." (Exodus 3:10)

The story of Moses begins in oppression. Israel is enslaved in Egypt, and Pharaoh seeks to destroy their future by killing male infants. Yet God preserves Moses - through a basket, a river, and the compassion of Pharaoh's daughter. Raised in royalty, Moses is called to lead a people out of bondage.

Preservation and Preparation

God's hand is evident from the beginning. Moses is rescued, raised in Pharaoh's court, and educated in Egyptian wisdom. But his heart remains with his people. After defending a Hebrew slave, he flees to Midian - where God begins to shape him in solitude.

  • Divine protection: A child preserved for a mission.
  • Identity struggle: Hebrew by birth, Egyptian by upbringing.
  • Wilderness training: Forty years of humility and preparation.

 

The Burning Bush and the Call

On Mount Horeb, God appears in fire. The bush burns but is not consumed - a symbol of holy presence and enduring purpose. God calls Moses by name and commissions him to confront Pharaoh. Moses resists, but God equips - providing signs, words, and Aaron as a helper.

This moment marks the beginning of Israel's deliverance and the unfolding of God's covenant faithfulness.

Relevance to Salvation

  • God raises deliverers from unlikely places.
  • Calling often comes through crisis and wilderness.
  • Salvation begins with God's initiative and empowering presence.
