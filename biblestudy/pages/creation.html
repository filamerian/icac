Creation and the Image of God (Genesis 1 - 2)

"Let us make man in our image, after our likeness..." (Genesis 1:26)

Genesis opens with a breathtaking declaration: "In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth." This is not just the start of the Bible-it is the beginning of purpose, order, and divine intention. God forms the world with rhythm and beauty, culminating in the creation of humanity.

Made in His Image

Human beings are not accidents of nature. We are image-bearers of the Creator, endowed with dignity, relational capacity, and spiritual purpose. This divine imprint is the foundation of our worth and the reason God seeks to redeem us.

  • Relational: We are made for communion-with God and one another.
  • Responsible: We are called to steward creation with wisdom and care.
  • Reflective: We mirror God's creativity, justice, and love.

 

The Blessing and the Mandate

God blesses humanity and commands them to "be fruitful and multiply, fill the earth and subdue it." This is not a burden-it is a calling. Salvation begins not with sin, but with divine blessing and purpose.

 

Genesis 2: Intimacy and Vocation

Genesis 2 zooms in on the creation of man and woman. God forms Adam from the dust and breathes life into him-a personal, intimate act. Then He places Adam in a garden, not just to enjoy, but to work and keep it. Salvation includes vocation: we are created to cultivate, protect, and steward.

 

Male and Female: Mutual Image-Bearers

God declares, "It is not good that the man should be alone." He creates Eve, not from dust, but from Adam's side signaling equality, partnership, and mutuality. Together, man and woman reflect the fullness of God's image.

 

Relevance to Salvation

  • We are created for relationship, not isolation.
  • God's plan for salvation begins with blessing, not punishment.
  • The image of God is marred by sin but restored through Christ.

"Put on the new self, which is being renewed in knowledge after the image of its Creator." (Colossians 3:10)
